Before I came to UTD, I imagined my exchange year as a non-stop parade of epic trips and wild parties. The reality, I’ve learned, is built on something much more valuable: a solid routine. It’s the framework that makes the adventures possible. So, I thought I’d pull back the curtain and show you what a typical week looks like for me here in Dallas.

Monday: The Productive Pause
My week starts with a gift: no classes. But this isn't a day for sleeping in. It’s my primary day to tackle the heavier Data Science homework and get ahead on weekly readings. I’ll usually camp out in the library for a few hours. It’s my day to set the tone for the week, ensuring my workload is managed before the fun begins.

Tuesday: The Marathon
Tuesdays are my longest days. It starts early with an 8 AM class, followed by a necessary gym session to wake me up properly. The real marathon is from 4 PM to 10 PM, where I’m back-to-back in labs and lectures. These are the intense, practical sessions I’ve talked about, and by the end, my brain is full, but it’s a satisfying kind of tired. Wednesday: The Reset
Another class-free day. The gym is a non-negotiable part of my routine, so I’ll hit it again in the morning. The rest of the day is for lighter work—reviewing notes, starting on smaller assignments, or running errands. It’s a crucial buffer day that keeps me from falling behind.

Thursday: The Hump Day
A split schedule. Another 8 AM class, another gym session. Then, I have a break before my afternoon class from 1 PM to 4 PM. This gap is perfect for a long lunch with friends or finishing up some pre-class prep. Once that last class ends, I can feel the weekend approaching.

Friday: The Finish Line
A single block of class from 10 AM to 1 PM. The energy on campus is completely different—everyone is ready for the weekend. Walking out of that building at 1 PM on a Friday is one of the best feelings of the week.

The Weekends: Freedom & Friends

  • Recovery: Catching up on sleep after a week of early mornings.
  • Socializing: Trying new restaurants, exploring Dallas, or hanging out with the other Spaniards.
  • Fun: The UTD gaming room is a frequent hangout spot.
  • Preparation: Sunday includes homework and studying to reset for Monday.

So, there you have it. It’s not all rodeos and road trips. The magic of this experience lies in this balance—the discipline of the weekdays fuels the freedom of the weekends, and it’s a rhythm I’ve come to love.
