Who am I? Arrival A Week in Dallas Texan Activities Halloween West Coast Roadtrip

If my first few weeks were about settling in, the last months have been about diving headfirst into the deep end of Texan culture. I’ve traded textbooks for playbooks and algorithms for arena lights, and let me tell you, the data I’ve collected on how Dallas has fun is incredibly compelling.

My American sports education began with a masterpiece of excess: AT&T Stadium. I went to see the Dallas Cowboys take on the Washington Commanders. The final score was a whopping 44-22 for the Cowboys, but the real story was the stadium itself. The place is a cathedral of football, and the massive, high-definition screen hanging from the ceiling feels like something from the future. The energy from the crowd was a different kind of passion than what I’m used to at a football (soccer) match, but it was just as electric.

From one kind of cowboy to another, my next stop was a real rodeo. This was a fascinating glimpse into a world I’d only ever seen in movies. I thought it was just about staying on a bucking bull, but there’s so much more to it. I was particularly amazed by the steer wrestling, where riders leap from their horses to wrestle young steers to the ground in a matter of seconds. Seeing the precision, timing, and raw bravery of these athletes up close was a completely new and unforgettable experience.

In the spirit of trying everything once, I visited a firing range. As someone from a country with very strict gun laws, this was a profound cultural experience. I started with a .22 caliber handgun and rifle, which were manageable. But then I tried a larger caliber handgun, and the sheer power and recoil of it was a physical shock I won’t soon forget. It was an interesting, if slightly intimidating, afternoon that gave me a very tangible understanding of a complex part of American life. But the absolute crown jewel of my sports streak was the NBA game. I saw the Dallas Mavericks take on the LA Clippers in the new In-Season Tournament. The game was absolutely insane—it went to double overtime! We got to witness a legendary performance, with James Harden dropping a 40-point triple-double. The back-and-forth intensity, the roar of the crowd with every clutch basket, and the sheer athleticism on display was unlike any live sporting event I’ve ever attended. It was pure, unscripted drama.

So, there you have it. I’ve gone from the gridiron to the dirt ring, from the firing line to the hardwood court. Each event has been a unique data point in understanding this place, and so far, the analysis shows that Texas knows how to put on a show.
