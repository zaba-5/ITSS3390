Who am I? Arrival A Week in Dallas Texan Activities Halloween West Coast Roadtrip

Well, I’ve been in Dallas for a few weeks now, and the initial whirlwind is finally starting to feel like a routine. My arrival was surprisingly smooth—a fellow UTD student was kind enough to pick me and my two friends up from the airport. That first drive was a blur of wide roads and big skies, a stark contrast to the dense cityscape I’m used to in Madrid. Speaking of my two friends from Spain, we’re all sharing a four-person dorm. It’s been great having them here; we’re navigating this new experience together, which makes the moments of confusion feel more like a shared adventure than a problem.

The biggest culture shock so far? The sheer size of everything. I’m used to walking everywhere or hopping on the metro in Madrid. Here, the campus is huge and the city is spread out. My phone is now permanently open to the Uber app. Getting a coffee or going to a store is a planned expedition, not a spontaneous stroll. It’s a different way of living that I’m still getting used to.

We’ve been lucky to find a little community here. Through a mix of luck and searching for familiar accents, we’ve met five other Spaniards at the university. It’s funny how you can travel 5,000 miles only to find yourself sitting in a Texan apartment, laughing about the same things you would back home. We spend a lot of time with them; it’s a comforting slice of home that makes the bigger adjustments feel smaller.

Academically, things are different, too. My Data Science classes at UTD are noticeably more practical from the get-go. There's less pure theory and more immediate application—we’re diving into coding projects and using real datasets already. It’s challenging in the best way, and it feels like I’m building tangible skills with every lecture.

So, the summary of my first few weeks: I’m living on Uber, my dorm is a basecamp for exploration, and I’m balancing new American experiences with the comfort of Spanish friends. The routine is forming, and I’m excited to see what comes next.
